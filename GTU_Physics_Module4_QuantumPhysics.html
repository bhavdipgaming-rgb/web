TOPIC 1

Wave-Particle Duality

1.1 de Broglie Hypothesis 🔥 Most Important

📖 WAVE-PARTICLE DUALITY

Light exhibits both wave properties (interference, diffraction) and particle properties (photoelectric effect, Compton effect). de Broglie proposed that matter also has dual nature.

⭐ de BROGLIE WAVELENGTH

Every moving particle has an associated wave with wavelength:

\[\lambda = \frac{h}{p} = \frac{h}{mv}\]

where h = Planck's constant (6.626×10⁻³⁴ J·s), p = momentum, m = mass, v = velocity

For electron accelerated through V volts:

\[\lambda = \frac{12.27}{\sqrt{V}} \text{ Å}\]
💡 WHY DON'T WE SEE WAVE NATURE IN DAILY LIFE? For macroscopic objects (large m), λ is extremely small. For a 1g object moving at 1 m/s: λ ≈ 10⁻³¹ m (undetectable). Wave nature is only observable for microscopic particles like electrons.

1.2 Davisson-Germer Experiment ⭐ High Priority

📖 DAVISSON-GERMER EXPERIMENT (1927)

First experimental verification of de Broglie's hypothesis. Electrons were diffracted by a nickel crystal, producing an interference pattern — proving electrons behave as waves.

🔧 EXPERIMENTAL SETUP
  • Electron gun produces beam of electrons (accelerated through V volts)
  • Electrons strike nickel crystal target
  • Scattered electrons detected at various angles
  • Maximum intensity at specific angle θ = 50° for V = 54V
  • Bragg's law satisfied: 2d sin θ = nλ

1.3 PYQs — Wave-Particle Duality 🔥 Most Important

🎯 ALL PREVIOUS YEAR QUESTIONS — WAVE-PARTICLE DUALITY

Q: State de Broglie hypothesis. Derive the expression for de Broglie wavelength.
Winter 2025 / Summer 2024 4 Marks 🔥 Most Important
💡 de Broglie extended wave-particle duality from light to matter.
HYPOTHESIS: Just as light exhibits both wave and particle properties, matter (electrons, protons, atoms) also has dual nature — both particle and wave characteristics.
DERIVATION: From Planck: E = hν (particle nature). From Einstein: E = mc². For photon: p = E/c = hν/c = h/λ
de Broglie's insight: If light (wave) has momentum p = h/λ, then matter (particle) should have wavelength λ = h/p
∴ λ = h/p = h/(mv) ✓ de Broglie wavelength formula
Q: Calculate the de Broglie wavelength of an electron accelerated through 100 V.
Summer 2025 3 Marks ⭐ Numerical
💡 Use the shortcut formula for electrons.
FORMULA: λ = 12.27/√V Å (for electron)
GIVEN: V = 100 volts
CALCULATION: λ = 12.27/√100 = 12.27/10 = 1.227 Å
∴ de Broglie wavelength = 1.227 Å = 0.1227 nm
Q: Describe Davisson-Germer experiment with neat diagram.
Winter 2024 4 Marks ⭐ Important
💡 Describe setup, observation, and conclusion.
SETUP: Electron gun → Nickel crystal → Detector (rotatable)
OBSERVATION: Maximum intensity at θ = 50° when V = 54V
CALCULATION: Using Bragg's law: 2d sin θ = nλ. For nickel d = 0.91 Å, θ = 50°, n = 1: λ = 2(0.91)(sin 50°) = 1.65 Å
VERIFICATION: de Broglie formula: λ = 12.27/√54 = 1.67 Å (matches!)
CONCLUSION: Electrons exhibit wave nature — confirmed de Broglie hypothesis
TOPIC 2

Heisenberg's Uncertainty Principle

2.1 Statement and Explanation 🔥 Most Important

📖 HEISENBERG'S UNCERTAINTY PRINCIPLE

It is impossible to measure both position and momentum of a particle simultaneously with unlimited precision.

Mathematical statement:

\[\Delta x \cdot \Delta p_x \geq \frac{h}{4\pi} = \frac{\hbar}{2}\]

where Δx = uncertainty in position, Δpₓ = uncertainty in momentum, ℏ = h/2π

💡 WHY DOES UNCERTAINTY EXIST? To measure position accurately, you need short-wavelength light (high energy photons). But high-energy photons disturb the particle's momentum. Better position measurement → worse momentum measurement, and vice versa.
⭐ OTHER UNCERTAINTY RELATIONS
PairRelation
Energy-TimeΔE · Δt ≥ ℏ/2
Angular momentum-AngleΔL · Δθ ≥ ℏ/2

2.2 Applications and Numericals ⭐ High Priority

🔧 WHY ELECTRON CANNOT EXIST IN NUCLEUS

Nuclear size ≈ 10⁻¹⁴ m. If electron confined in nucleus, Δx ≈ 10⁻¹⁴ m.

From uncertainty principle: Δp ≥ ℏ/(2Δx) ≈ 5×10⁻²¹ kg·m/s

Corresponding energy E ≈ 100 MeV (much larger than observed beta decay energies ~3 MeV)

∴ Electron cannot be confined in nucleus!

2.3 PYQs — Uncertainty Principle 🔥 Most Important

🎯 ALL PREVIOUS YEAR QUESTIONS — UNCERTAINTY PRINCIPLE

Q: State and explain Heisenberg's uncertainty principle.
Winter 2025 / Summer 2025 3 Marks 🔥 Repeated
💡 State principle, give formula, explain significance.
STATEMENT: It is impossible to determine simultaneously both the position and momentum of a particle with unlimited precision.
MATHEMATICAL FORM: Δx · Δp ≥ ℏ/2 where ℏ = h/2π = 1.055×10⁻³⁴ J·s
EXPLANATION: The more precisely we know position (small Δx), the less precisely we can know momentum (large Δp), and vice versa.
This is a fundamental property of nature, not a limitation of measurement instruments.
Q: An electron is confined to a nucleus of size 10⁻¹⁴ m. Calculate the minimum uncertainty in its velocity.
Winter 2024 4 Marks ⭐ Numerical
💡 Use Δx · Δp ≥ ℏ/2 and Δp = mΔv.
GIVEN: Δx = 10⁻¹⁴ m; m_e = 9.1×10⁻³¹ kg; ℏ = 1.055×10⁻³⁴ J·s
STEP 1: Δx · Δp ≥ ℏ/2 ⇒ Δp ≥ ℏ/(2Δx)
STEP 2: Δp = m·Δv, so Δv ≥ ℏ/(2mΔx)
STEP 3: Δv ≥ (1.055×10⁻³⁴)/(2 × 9.1×10⁻³¹ × 10⁻¹⁴)
STEP 4: Δv ≥ 5.8×10⁹ m/s
∴ Minimum uncertainty in velocity ≈ 5.8×10⁹ m/s (greater than speed of light! Proves electron can't exist in nucleus)
TOPIC 3

Schrodinger Equation

3.1 Time-Independent Schrodinger Equation 🔥 Most Important

📖 SCHRODINGER EQUATION

The fundamental equation of quantum mechanics that describes how the quantum state of a physical system changes.

⭐ TIME-INDEPENDENT SCHRODINGER EQUATION (1D) \[-\frac{\hbar^2}{2m}\frac{d^2\psi}{dx^2} + V(x)\psi = E\psi\]

where:
ψ = wave function
V(x) = potential energy
E = total energy
m = mass of particle

Physical significance of |ψ|²: Probability density of finding particle at position x

📖 CONDITIONS ON WAVE FUNCTION
  • ψ must be finite and single-valued
  • ψ must be continuous
  • dψ/dx must be continuous
  • ∫|ψ|²dx = 1 (normalization condition)

3.2 Particle in a Box ⭐ High Priority

⭐ PARTICLE IN A 1D BOX (Infinite Potential Well)

For particle of mass m in box of width L:

Energy levels (quantized):

\[E_n = \frac{n^2h^2}{8mL^2} \quad n = 1, 2, 3, ...\]

Wave functions:

\[\psi_n(x) = \sqrt{\frac{2}{L}}\sin\left(\frac{n\pi x}{L}\right)\]

Note: n = 0 is NOT allowed — ground state is n = 1 (zero-point energy)

💡 KEY CONCEPTS
  • Energy is quantized (only discrete values allowed)
  • Lowest energy E₁ = h²/(8mL²) ≠ 0 (zero-point energy)
  • Particle cannot be at rest (uncertainty principle)
  • Probability of finding particle varies with position

3.3 PYQs — Schrodinger Equation 🔥 Most Important

🎯 ALL PREVIOUS YEAR QUESTIONS — SCHRODINGER EQUATION

Q: Write the time-independent Schrodinger equation and explain the physical significance of wave function ψ.
Winter 2025 / Summer 2024 4 Marks 🔥 Important
💡 Write equation + Born interpretation of ψ.
SCHRODINGER EQUATION (1D): -ℏ²/(2m) · d²ψ/dx² + V(x)ψ = Eψ
PHYSICAL SIGNIFICANCE OF ψ:

  • ψ itself has no direct physical meaning (can be complex)
  • |ψ|² represents probability density
  • Probability of finding particle between x and x+dx = |ψ|²dx
  • ∫|ψ|²dx = 1 (total probability = 1, normalization)
ψ contains all information about the quantum state of the particle.
Q: Derive the expression for energy of a particle in a 1D box of width L.
Winter 2024 7 Marks 🔥 Most Important
💡 Solve Schrodinger equation with boundary conditions ψ(0) = ψ(L) = 0.
STEP 1: Inside box: V = 0. Schrodinger equation: -ℏ²/(2m) · d²ψ/dx² = Eψ
STEP 2: Rearrange: d²ψ/dx² + (2mE/ℏ²)ψ = 0
STEP 3: Let k² = 2mE/ℏ². General solution: ψ = A sin(kx) + B cos(kx)
STEP 4: Boundary condition ψ(0) = 0 ⇒ B = 0. So ψ = A sin(kx)
STEP 5: Boundary condition ψ(L) = 0 ⇒ sin(kL) = 0 ⇒ kL = nπ (n = 1,2,3...)
STEP 6: k = nπ/L. Substitute back: 2mE/ℏ² = n²π²/L²
∴ E_n = n²π²ℏ²/(2mL²) = n²h²/(8mL²) ✓
Q: An electron is confined in a 1D box of width 1 Å. Calculate its ground state energy.
Summer 2025 3 Marks ⭐ Numerical
GIVEN: L = 1 Å = 10⁻¹⁰ m; m = 9.1×10⁻³¹ kg; h = 6.626×10⁻³⁴ J·s; n = 1 (ground state)
FORMULA: E₁ = h²/(8mL²)
CALCULATION: E₁ = (6.626×10⁻³⁴)² / (8 × 9.1×10⁻³¹ × (10⁻¹⁰)²)
RESULT: E₁ = 6.02×10⁻¹⁸ J = 37.6 eV
∴ Ground state energy = 37.6 eV
