TOPIC 1

Interference of Light

1.1 Young's Double Slit Experiment 🔥 Most Important

📖 INTERFERENCE

When two coherent light waves superpose, the resultant intensity is redistributed. At some points intensity is maximum (constructive), at others minimum (destructive).

Conditions for interference:

  • Sources must be coherent (constant phase difference)
  • Same frequency/wavelength
  • Same amplitude (for good contrast)
⭐ YOUNG'S DOUBLE SLIT FORMULAS
QuantityFormula
Path differenceΔ = d sin θ ≈ dy/D (for small θ)
Constructive (bright)Δ = nλ, where n = 0, 1, 2, ...
Destructive (dark)Δ = (2n+1)λ/2
Fringe widthβ = λD/d

where d = slit separation, D = screen distance, λ = wavelength

1.2 Thin Film Interference 🔥 Most Important

💡 WHY FILMS SHOW COLORS When light reflects from top and bottom surfaces of a thin film (soap bubble, oil on water), the two reflected waves interfere. Different wavelengths interfere constructively at different thicknesses, creating colorful patterns.
⭐ CONDITIONS FOR THIN FILM (Reflected Light)

For film of thickness t and refractive index μ, with light incident at angle r:

ConditionFormula
Constructive (bright)2μt cos r = (2n+1)λ/2
Destructive (dark)2μt cos r = nλ

Note: Extra λ/2 path difference due to reflection from denser medium!

⭐ NEWTON'S RINGS

When a plano-convex lens is placed on a glass plate, circular interference fringes (Newton's rings) are formed.

For reflected light:

  • Dark rings: D_n² = 4nλR (n = 0, 1, 2, ...)
  • Bright rings: D_n² = 4(n+½)λR

where R = radius of curvature of lens

1.3 PYQs — Interference 🔥 Most Important

🎯 ALL PREVIOUS YEAR QUESTIONS — INTERFERENCE

Q: Explain Young's double slit experiment. Derive the expression for fringe width.
Winter 2025 / Summer 2024 7 Marks 🔥 Most Important
💡 Standard derivation with diagram description.
SETUP: Two slits S₁, S₂ separated by distance d. Screen at distance D. Monochromatic light of wavelength λ.
STEP 1: Path difference at point P (distance y from center): Δ = S₂P - S₁P
STEP 2: From geometry: Δ = d sin θ ≈ dy/D (for small θ)
STEP 3: For bright fringes: Δ = nλ ⇒ dy/D = nλ ⇒ y_n = nλD/d
STEP 4: For dark fringes: Δ = (2n+1)λ/2 ⇒ y'_n = (2n+1)λD/(2d)
STEP 5: Fringe width β = distance between consecutive bright fringes
STEP 6: β = y_{n+1} - y_n = (n+1)λD/d - nλD/d = λD/d
∴ Fringe width β = λD/d
Q: Derive the conditions for constructive and destructive interference in thin films (reflected light).
Summer 2025 4 Marks 🔥 Important
💡 Consider path difference between rays reflected from top and bottom surfaces.
STEP 1: Ray 1 reflects from top surface (air→film). Ray 2 reflects from bottom surface (film→air).
STEP 2: Path difference due to travel in film = 2μt cos r
STEP 3: Additional λ/2 path difference because Ray 1 reflects from denser medium
STEP 4: Total path difference: Δ = 2μt cos r + λ/2
STEP 5: For constructive: Δ = nλ ⇒ 2μt cos r = (2n-1)λ/2
STEP 6: For destructive: Δ = (2n+1)λ/2 ⇒ 2μt cos r = nλ
∴ Bright: 2μt cos r = (2n+1)λ/2; Dark: 2μt cos r = nλ
Q: In Newton's rings experiment, prove that diameter of nth dark ring is proportional to √n.
Winter 2024 4 Marks ⭐ Important
STEP 1: For nth dark ring in reflected light: 2μt cos r = nλ
STEP 2: For air film (μ=1) and normal incidence (cos r = 1): 2t = nλ
STEP 3: From geometry of lens: t = r²/(2R) where r = radius of ring, R = lens curvature radius
STEP 4: Substituting: 2(r²/2R) = nλ ⇒ r² = nλR
STEP 5: Diameter D_n = 2r, so D_n² = 4r² = 4nλR
∴ D_n = 2√(nλR) ∝ √n ✓ Proved
TOPIC 2

Diffraction of Light

2.1 Fraunhofer Diffraction at Single Slit 🔥 Most Important

📖 DIFFRACTION

The bending of light around obstacles and spreading into the geometric shadow region is called diffraction.

Types:

  • Fraunhofer: Source and screen at infinity (parallel rays)
  • Fresnel: Source or screen at finite distance
⭐ SINGLE SLIT DIFFRACTION PATTERN

For slit of width a, wavelength λ:

ConditionFormula
Minima (dark)a sin θ = nλ, n = ±1, ±2, ...
Secondary maximaa sin θ = (2n+1)λ/2
Central maximum widthW = 2λD/a

Note: Central maximum is twice as wide as secondary maxima!

2.2 Diffraction Grating ⭐ High Priority

📖 DIFFRACTION GRATING

A grating consists of a large number of parallel slits of equal width separated by equal opaque spaces.

Grating element: d = a + b (slit width + opaque space)

Grating constant: N = number of lines per unit length = 1/d

⭐ GRATING EQUATION

For principal maxima:

\[d \sin \theta = n\lambda\]

where n = order of spectrum (0, ±1, ±2, ...)

For normal incidence: (a+b) sin θ = nλ

2.3 PYQs — Diffraction 🔥 Most Important

🎯 ALL PREVIOUS YEAR QUESTIONS — DIFFRACTION

Q: Explain Fraunhofer diffraction at a single slit. Derive the condition for minima.
Winter 2025 7 Marks 🔥 Most Important
💡 Use phasor method or path difference approach.
SETUP: Parallel beam of wavelength λ incident on slit of width a. Diffracted light focused on screen by lens.
STEP 1: Consider secondary wavelets from all points in slit. At angle θ, path difference between wavelets from top and bottom edges = a sin θ
STEP 2: Divide slit into two halves. For first minimum, path difference between corresponding points in two halves = λ/2
STEP 3: Total path difference = a sin θ = λ (for first minimum, n=1)
STEP 4: General condition: Path difference = nλ for nth minimum
∴ Condition for minima: a sin θ = nλ, where n = ±1, ±2, ...
Q: Derive the grating equation for a plane diffraction grating.
Summer 2024 4 Marks ⭐ Important
STEP 1: Consider grating with slit separation d (grating element)
STEP 2: For light diffracted at angle θ, path difference between adjacent slits = d sin θ
STEP 3: For constructive interference (principal maxima), path difference = nλ
∴ d sin θ = nλ ✓ where n = 0, ±1, ±2, ...
TOPIC 3

Polarization of Light

3.1 Methods of Polarization ⭐ High Priority

📖 POLARIZATION

The phenomenon of confining light vibrations to a particular direction perpendicular to propagation is called polarization.

Evidence: Polarization proves light is a transverse wave (longitudinal waves cannot be polarized).

📖 METHODS OF POLARIZATION
MethodDescription
ReflectionLight reflected at Brewster's angle is completely polarized
RefractionMultiple refractions through pile of plates
Selective AbsorptionPolaroids absorb one component, transmit other
ScatteringSky light is partially polarized due to scattering
Double RefractionBirefringent crystals split light into O-ray and E-ray

3.2 Malus's Law and Brewster's Law 🔥 Most Important

⭐ MALUS'S LAW

When polarized light passes through an analyzer, the transmitted intensity varies as:

\[I = I_0 \cos^2\theta\]

where I₀ = incident intensity, θ = angle between polarizer and analyzer axes

⭐ BREWSTER'S LAW

When unpolarized light is incident at polarizing angle (Brewster's angle) i_p:

\[\tan i_p = \mu\]

where μ = refractive index of medium

At Brewster's angle: Reflected and refracted rays are perpendicular (i_p + r = 90°)

3.3 PYQs — Polarization 🔥 Most Important

🎯 ALL PREVIOUS YEAR QUESTIONS — POLARIZATION

Q: State and explain Malus's law of polarization.
Winter 2025 / Summer 2025 3 Marks 🔥 Repeated
💡 Direct statement with formula.
STATEMENT: When a beam of completely plane-polarized light is passed through an analyzer, the intensity of transmitted light varies as the square of the cosine of the angle between the transmission axes of polarizer and analyzer.
FORMULA: I = I₀ cos²θ
SPECIAL CASES: θ = 0°: I = I₀ (max); θ = 90°: I = 0 (min)
Write statement + formula + special cases for full marks.
Q: Derive Brewster's law and explain its significance.
Winter 2024 4 Marks ⭐ Important
💡 Use geometry and Snell's law.
STEP 1: At polarizing angle i_p, reflected light is completely polarized
STEP 2: Reflected and refracted rays are perpendicular: i_p + r = 90°
STEP 3: From Snell's law: μ = sin i_p / sin r
STEP 4: Since r = 90° - i_p: sin r = sin(90° - i_p) = cos i_p
STEP 5: Therefore: μ = sin i_p / cos i_p = tan i_p
∴ tan i_p = μ ✓ Brewster's law
Q: Two polaroids are placed with their axes at 60°. If unpolarized light of intensity I₀ is incident, find the transmitted intensity.
Summer 2024 3 Marks ⭐ Numerical
STEP 1: After first polaroid: I₁ = I₀/2 (unpolarized → polarized)
STEP 2: After second polaroid (analyzer): I₂ = I₁ cos²θ
STEP 3: θ = 60°, so cos 60° = 0.5, cos²60° = 0.25
STEP 4: I₂ = (I₀/2) × 0.25 = I₀/8
∴ Transmitted intensity = I₀/8
