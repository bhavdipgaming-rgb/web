TOPIC 1

Basic Laser Theory

1.1 Absorption, Spontaneous and Stimulated Emission 🔥 Most Important

📖 LASER

LASER = Light Amplification by Stimulated Emission of Radiation

Characteristics of Laser Light:

  • Monochromatic: Single wavelength (very narrow bandwidth)
  • Coherent: Waves are in phase (temporal and spatial coherence)
  • Directional: Very low divergence (collimated beam)
  • High Intensity: Highly concentrated energy
📖 THREE ATOMIC PROCESSES
ProcessDescriptionDiagram
AbsorptionAtom absorbs photon, goes from E₁ to E₂E₁ + hν → E₂
Spontaneous EmissionAtom randomly drops from E₂ to E₁, emits photonE₂ → E₁ + hν
Stimulated EmissionIncident photon triggers emission of identical photonE₂ + hν → E₁ + 2hν
💡 KEY DIFFERENCE

Spontaneous emission: Random, incoherent light (LED, tube light)
Stimulated emission: Triggered, coherent light (LASER) — emitted photon has same phase, frequency, polarization, and direction as incident photon!

1.2 Einstein's A and B Coefficients 🔥 Most Important

⭐ EINSTEIN'S COEFFICIENTS

Einstein (1917) introduced coefficients to describe the three processes:

CoefficientProcessRate
A₂₁Spontaneous emissionRate = A₂₁N₂
B₁₂AbsorptionRate = B₁₂N₁ρ(ν)
B₂₁Stimulated emissionRate = B₂₁N₂ρ(ν)

where N₁, N₂ = populations of lower and upper states, ρ(ν) = energy density of radiation

⭐ RATIO OF EINSTEIN COEFFICIENTS

At thermal equilibrium, rate of upward transitions = rate of downward transitions:

\[\frac{A_{21}}{B_{21}} = \frac{8\pi h\nu^3}{c^3}\]

Key conclusions:

  • A₂₁/B₂₁ ∝ ν³ — spontaneous emission dominates at high frequencies
  • For laser action, we need stimulated emission to dominate → need high N₂ (population inversion)

1.3 PYQs — Basic Laser Theory 🔥 Most Important

🎯 ALL PREVIOUS YEAR QUESTIONS — LASER THEORY

Q: Explain the processes of spontaneous and stimulated emission of radiation.
Winter 2025 / Summer 2024 4 Marks 🔥 Most Important
💡 Contrast the two emission processes clearly.
SPONTANEOUS EMISSION: An excited atom in higher energy state E₂ randomly (without external trigger) drops to lower state E₁, emitting a photon of energy hν = E₂ - E₁.
Characteristics: Random process, photons emitted in random directions, different phases → INCOHERENT light
STIMULATED EMISSION: When an excited atom is struck by an incident photon of energy hν = E₂ - E₁, it is stimulated to drop to E₁ and emit a second photon.
Characteristics: Triggered process, emitted photon is IDENTICAL to incident photon (same frequency, phase, polarization, direction) → COHERENT light
Stimulated emission is the basis of LASER action. One photon → two photons → four photons (chain reaction = amplification).
Q: Derive the relationship between Einstein's A and B coefficients.
Winter 2024 7 Marks 🔥 Important
💡 Consider thermal equilibrium between radiation and atoms.
STEP 1: At equilibrium, rate of absorption = rate of emission
STEP 2: Rate of absorption (E₁→E₂) = B₁₂N₁ρ(ν)
STEP 3: Rate of emission (E₂→E₁) = A₂₁N₂ + B₂₁N₂ρ(ν) (spontaneous + stimulated)
STEP 4: At equilibrium: B₁₂N₁ρ = A₂₁N₂ + B₂₁N₂ρ
STEP 5: From Boltzmann distribution: N₂/N₁ = e^(-hν/kT)
STEP 6: Solving: ρ(ν) = A₂₁ / [B₁₂e^(hν/kT) - B₂₁]
STEP 7: Compare with Planck's radiation law: ρ(ν) = (8πhν³/c³) · 1/(e^(hν/kT) - 1)
∴ A₂₁/B₂₁ = 8πhν³/c³ and B₁₂ = B₂₁ ✓
TOPIC 2

Population Inversion and Pumping

2.1 Metastable States and Population Inversion 🔥 Most Important

📖 POPULATION INVERSION

Under normal conditions (thermal equilibrium), N₁ > N₂ (more atoms in lower energy state). Population inversion is the condition where N₂ > N₁ (more atoms in excited state than ground state).

This is essential for laser action!

📖 METASTABLE STATE

A metastable state is an excited energy state with unusually long lifetime (10⁻³ s compared to 10⁻⁸ s for normal excited states).

Importance: Atoms can "accumulate" in metastable state, making population inversion possible.

⭐ CONDITIONS FOR LASER ACTION
  • Population inversion: N₂ > N₁ (necessary for net stimulated emission)
  • Metastable state: Long lifetime allows accumulation of excited atoms
  • Optical resonator: Mirrors provide feedback for amplification
  • Pumping mechanism: External energy source to create population inversion

2.2 Pumping Methods ⭐ High Priority

📖 PUMPING

The process of supplying energy to raise atoms from lower energy state to higher energy state is called pumping.

📖 TYPES OF PUMPING
MethodDescriptionExample
Optical PumpingUsing intense light source (flash lamp)Ruby laser
Electrical DischargeElectric current through gasHe-Ne laser, CO₂ laser
Direct ConversionElectrical → optical (semiconductor)Diode laser
Chemical PumpingEnergy from chemical reactionChemical lasers
Gas DynamicRapid cooling of hot gasGas dynamic CO₂ laser
💡 THREE-LEVEL vs FOUR-LEVEL LASERS

Three-level (Ruby): Ground state → excited → metastable → ground. Hard to achieve inversion (need >50% atoms excited).

Four-level (He-Ne, Nd:YAG): Extra level below metastable. Easy to achieve inversion (lower laser level is nearly empty).

2.3 PYQs — Population Inversion 🔥 Most Important

🎯 ALL PREVIOUS YEAR QUESTIONS — POPULATION INVERSION

Q: What is population inversion? Why is it necessary for laser action?
Winter 2025 / Summer 2025 4 Marks 🔥 Repeated
💡 Explain normal distribution vs inverted distribution.
DEFINITION: Population inversion is the condition where more atoms are in higher energy state (E₂) than in lower energy state (E₁), i.e., N₂ > N₁.
NORMAL CONDITION: At thermal equilibrium, N₁ >> N₂ (Boltzmann distribution). Absorption dominates over stimulated emission.
WHY NECESSARY: For laser amplification, stimulated emission must exceed absorption. This requires N₂ > N₁. Without population inversion, incident photons are absorbed rather than causing stimulated emission.
Population inversion is the fundamental requirement for laser action — it ensures net light amplification rather than absorption.
Q: Explain the role of metastable state in achieving population inversion.
Winter 2024 3 Marks ⭐ Important
METASTABLE STATE: An excited state with long lifetime (~10⁻³ s vs normal 10⁻⁸ s).
ROLE: Atoms pumped to higher energy quickly decay to metastable state. Due to long lifetime, atoms accumulate in metastable state. This makes N₂ > N₁ possible.
Without metastable state, atoms would decay too quickly for population inversion to build up.
TOPIC 3

Laser Systems and Applications

3.1 Ruby Laser and He-Ne Laser 🔥 Most Important

📖 RUBY LASER (First Laser, 1960)

Active medium: Ruby crystal (Al₂O₃ doped with Cr³⁺ ions)
Type: Three-level solid-state laser
Wavelength: 694.3 nm (red light)
Pumping: Optical (xenon flash lamp)
Output: Pulsed (not continuous)

🔧 RUBY LASER WORKING
  1. Flash lamp excites Cr³⁺ ions from ground state E₁ to broad band E₃
  2. Rapid non-radiative decay to metastable state E₂ (lifetime 3 ms)
  3. Population inversion builds between E₂ and E₁
  4. Spontaneous emission triggers stimulated emission
  5. Photons bounce between mirrored ends, amplifying
  6. Intense red pulse emerges from partially silvered end
📖 He-Ne LASER (Gas Laser)

Active medium: Helium-Neon gas mixture (10:1 ratio)
Type: Four-level gas laser
Wavelength: 632.8 nm (red), also 1.15 μm, 3.39 μm
Pumping: Electrical discharge
Output: Continuous wave (CW)

🔧 He-Ne LASER WORKING
  1. Electric discharge excites He atoms to metastable states
  2. He collides with Ne, transfers energy (resonant transfer)
  3. Ne atoms excited to upper laser level
  4. Population inversion created in Ne (not He!)
  5. Stimulated emission produces 632.8 nm light
  6. Mirrors provide feedback for continuous output

🎯 ALL PREVIOUS YEAR QUESTIONS — LASER SYSTEMS

Q: Explain the construction and working of Ruby laser with energy level diagram.
Winter 2025 / Summer 2024 7 Marks 🔥 Most Important
💡 Three-level laser system. Describe components + energy transitions.
CONSTRUCTION: Ruby rod (Al₂O₃ + 0.05% Cr³⁺), both ends polished and silvered (one fully, one partially). Xenon flash lamp for pumping.
ENERGY LEVELS: E₁ (ground), E₂ (metastable, 1.79 eV), E₃ (broad band, 3.0 eV)
WORKING — Step 1: Flash lamp excites Cr³⁺ from E₁ to E₃ (absorption band)
Step 2: Rapid non-radiative decay E₃ → E₂ (releases heat)
Step 3: Atoms accumulate in E₂ (metastable, 3 ms lifetime)
Step 4: Population inversion achieved between E₂ and E₁
Step 5: Spontaneous emission triggers chain reaction of stimulated emission
Step 6: Photons reflect between mirrors, amplifying
OUTPUT: 694.3 nm (red) pulsed laser beam from partially silvered end.
Q: Describe the construction and working of He-Ne laser.
Winter 2024 7 Marks 🔥 Important
💡 Four-level laser. He atoms assist, Ne atoms lase.
CONSTRUCTION: Discharge tube with He:Ne gas (10:1 ratio) at ~1 torr. Electrodes for electric discharge. Mirrors at ends (one partially transparent).
ENERGY LEVELS: He has metastable states at 20.61 eV and 19.82 eV. Ne has matching levels at 20.66 eV and 19.78 eV.
WORKING — Step 1: Electric discharge excites He atoms to metastable states
Step 2: Excited He collides with ground-state Ne, transfers energy (resonant energy transfer)
Step 3: Ne excited to upper laser level; He returns to ground state
Step 4: Population inversion in Ne (lower laser level is nearly empty)
Step 5: Stimulated emission at 632.8 nm (visible red)
OUTPUT: Continuous 632.8 nm red laser beam. Advantages: stable, coherent, low cost.
