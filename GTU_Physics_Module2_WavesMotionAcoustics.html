TOPIC 1

Simple Harmonic Motion (SHM)

1.1 Definition and Differential Equation 🔥 Most Important

📖 SIMPLE HARMONIC MOTION

A particle executes SHM when it oscillates about a mean position such that:

  • The restoring force is proportional to displacement
  • The force is always directed towards the mean position

Mathematical condition: F = -kx (Hooke's law type force)

⭐ DIFFERENTIAL EQUATION OF SHM

From Newton's 2nd law: F = ma = m(d²x/dt²)

For SHM: F = -kx

Therefore: \[\frac{d^2x}{dt^2} + \omega^2 x = 0\]

where ω² = k/m is the angular frequency

Solution: x = A sin(ωt + φ) or x = A cos(ωt + φ)

📖 KEY TERMS IN SHM
TermDefinitionFormula
Amplitude (A)Maximum displacement from mean position—
Time Period (T)Time for one complete oscillationT = 2π/ω = 2π√(m/k)
Frequency (n)Oscillations per secondn = 1/T = ω/(2π)
Phase (ωt+φ)Specifies position in cycleφ = initial phase

1.2 Velocity, Acceleration and Energy in SHM ⭐ High Priority

⭐ VELOCITY AND ACCELERATION

For x = A sin(ωt + φ):

QuantityFormulaMaximum Value
Displacementx = A sin(ωt + φ)A
Velocityv = dx/dt = Aω cos(ωt + φ)Aω
Accelerationa = dv/dt = -Aω² sin(ωt + φ)Aω²

Phase relationships:

  • Velocity leads displacement by π/2 (90°)
  • Acceleration leads velocity by π/2 (90°)
  • Acceleration is opposite to displacement (180° out of phase)
⭐ ENERGY IN SHM
Energy TypeFormula
Kinetic EnergyKE = ½mv² = ½mω²(A²-x²) = ½k(A²-x²)
Potential EnergyPE = ½kx² = ½mω²x²
Total EnergyE = KE + PE = ½kA² = ½mω²A² (constant!)

Note: Total energy is conserved. KE is max at mean position, PE is max at extreme position.

1.3 PYQs — Simple Harmonic Motion 🔥 Most Important

🎯 ALL PREVIOUS YEAR QUESTIONS — SHM

Q: Define Simple Harmonic Motion. Derive the differential equation of SHM.
Winter 2025 / Summer 2024 4 Marks 🔥 Most Important
💡 Definition + derivation from basic principles.
DEFINITION: SHM is oscillatory motion where restoring force is proportional to displacement and directed towards mean position.
STEP 1: By definition: F ∝ -x ⇒ F = -kx (where k = force constant)
STEP 2: From Newton's 2nd law: F = ma = m(d²x/dt²)
STEP 3: Equating: m(d²x/dt²) = -kx
STEP 4: Rearranging: d²x/dt² = -(k/m)x
STEP 5: Let ω² = k/m, then:
∴ d²x/dt² + ω²x = 0 ✓ This is the differential equation of SHM
Q: Show that total energy in SHM is conserved (constant).
Summer 2025 / Winter 2023 4 Marks 🔥 Important
💡 Add KE and PE, show sum is independent of time.
STEP 1: For x = A sin(ωt + φ), velocity v = Aω cos(ωt + φ)
STEP 2: Kinetic Energy: KE = ½mv² = ½m(Aω cos(ωt+φ))² = ½mA²ω²cos²(ωt+φ)
STEP 3: Potential Energy: PE = ½kx² = ½k(A sin(ωt+φ))² = ½kA²sin²(ωt+φ)
STEP 4: Since k = mω²: PE = ½mω²A²sin²(ωt+φ)
STEP 5: Total Energy: E = KE + PE = ½mA²ω²[cos²(ωt+φ) + sin²(ωt+φ)]
STEP 6: Using identity: cos²θ + sin²θ = 1
∴ E = ½mA²ω² (constant, independent of time) ✓ Energy is conserved
Q: A particle in SHM has amplitude 5 cm and period 2 s. Find maximum velocity and maximum acceleration.
Winter 2024 3 Marks ⭐ Numerical
💡 Direct formula application.
GIVEN: A = 5 cm = 0.05 m; T = 2 s
STEP 1: ω = 2π/T = 2π/2 = π rad/s
STEP 2: v_max = Aω = 0.05 × π = 0.157 m/s
STEP 3: a_max = Aω² = 0.05 × π² = 0.05 × 9.87 = 0.493 m/s²
∴ Maximum velocity = 0.157 m/s; Maximum acceleration = 0.493 m/s²
TOPIC 2

Wave Motion and Superposition

2.1 Wave Equation and Types of Waves 🔥 Most Important

📖 WAVE MOTION

Wave motion is the transfer of energy through a medium without actual transfer of matter. Particles oscillate about their mean positions while energy propagates forward.

⭐ ONE-DIMENSIONAL WAVE EQUATION

\[\frac{\partial^2 y}{\partial x^2} = \frac{1}{v^2}\frac{\partial^2 y}{\partial t^2}\]

where v = wave velocity

General solution: y = f(x-vt) + g(x+vt)

Sinusoidal wave: y = A sin(kx - ωt) for wave traveling in +x direction

where k = 2π/λ (wave number), ω = 2πn (angular frequency)

📖 TYPES OF WAVES
TypeParticle MotionExample
TransversePerpendicular to wave directionString waves, EM waves
LongitudinalParallel to wave directionSound waves in air

2.2 Principle of Superposition ⭐ High Priority

📖 PRINCIPLE OF SUPERPOSITION

When two or more waves overlap in a medium, the resultant displacement at any point is the vector sum of individual displacements at that point.

Mathematically: y = y₁ + y₂ + y₃ + ...

💡 APPLICATIONS OF SUPERPOSITION
  • Interference: Two waves of same frequency → constructive/destructive interference
  • Beats: Two waves of slightly different frequencies → periodic variation in amplitude
  • Standing Waves: Two identical waves traveling in opposite directions → nodes and antinodes
⭐ BEATS FREQUENCY

When two waves of frequencies n₁ and n₂ (n₁ ≈ n₂) superpose:

Beat frequency: n_beat = |n₁ - n₂|

Application: Tuning musical instruments, detecting frequency differences

2.3 PYQs — Wave Motion 🔥 Most Important

🎯 ALL PREVIOUS YEAR QUESTIONS — WAVE MOTION

Q: Derive the one-dimensional wave equation and write its general solution.
Summer 2025 4 Marks 🔥 Important
💡 Consider transverse wave on a stretched string.
STEP 1: Consider a small element dx of a stretched string under tension T.
STEP 2: Net transverse force = T[sin(θ+dθ) - sin(θ)] ≈ T·dθ (for small angles)
STEP 3: For small slopes: tan θ ≈ ∂y/∂x, so net force = T(∂²y/∂x²)dx
STEP 4: From Newton's law: (μ·dx)(∂²y/∂t²) = T(∂²y/∂x²)dx
STEP 5: Simplifying: ∂²y/∂t² = (T/μ)(∂²y/∂x²)
STEP 6: Wave velocity v = √(T/μ), so:
∴ ∂²y/∂x² = (1/v²)(∂²y/∂t²) ✓ General solution: y = f(x-vt) + g(x+vt)
Q: Explain the phenomenon of beats with theory.
Winter 2024 4 Marks ⭐ Important
💡 Superposition of two waves with slightly different frequencies.
STEP 1: Let y₁ = A sin(ω₁t) and y₂ = A sin(ω₂t) be two waves
STEP 2: By superposition: y = y₁ + y₂ = A[sin(ω₁t) + sin(ω₂t)]
STEP 3: Using identity: sin C + sin D = 2 cos((C-D)/2) sin((C+D)/2)
STEP 4: y = 2A cos((ω₁-ω₂)t/2) · sin((ω₁+ω₂)t/2)
STEP 5: Amplitude varies as 2A cos((ω₁-ω₂)t/2)
STEP 6: Beat frequency = |n₁ - n₂| = |(ω₁-ω₂)/(2π)|
∴ Beats are periodic variations in loudness with frequency = |n₁ - n₂|
TOPIC 3

Acoustics and Ultrasonics

3.1 Reverberation Time and Sabine's Formula 🔥 Most Important

📖 REVERBERATION TIME (T)

The time taken for sound intensity to decrease to one-millionth (10⁻⁶) of its original value after the source is stopped.

⭐ SABINE'S FORMULA

\[T = \frac{0.161V}{\sum a_i S_i} = \frac{0.161V}{A}\]

where:
V = volume of hall (m³)
S_i = surface area of each surface (m²)
a_i = absorption coefficient of each surface
A = total absorption = Σa_iS_i (in m² sabine or OWU)

🔧 CONDITIONS FOR GOOD ACOUSTICS
  • Reverberation time should be optimum (1-2 seconds for concert halls)
  • Uniform distribution of sound
  • No echoes or focusing of sound
  • Absence of external noise

3.2 Ultrasonics and Applications ⭐ High Priority

📖 ULTRASONICS

Sound waves with frequency above 20 kHz (beyond human hearing) are called ultrasonics.

📖 PRODUCTION METHODS
MethodPrincipleFrequency Range
MagnetostrictionChange in dimensions of ferromagnetic material in magnetic fieldUp to 30 kHz
PiezoelectricDeformation of piezoelectric crystal under electric fieldUp to 500 MHz
💡 APPLICATIONS OF ULTRASONICS
  • Sonar (underwater navigation and detection)
  • Ultrasonic cleaning
  • Medical imaging (ultrasound scans)
  • Non-destructive testing (crack detection)
  • Ultrasonic welding

🎯 ALL PREVIOUS YEAR QUESTIONS — ACOUSTICS

Q: Derive Sabine's formula for reverberation time.
Winter 2025 / Summer 2024 7 Marks 🔥 Most Important
💡 Consider growth and decay of sound energy in an enclosed space.
STEP 1: Let E = total sound energy in hall, V = volume, S = total surface area
STEP 2: Rate of energy loss = rate at which energy hits walls × absorption coefficient
STEP 3: Energy density = E/V. Energy flowing to wall per unit area per unit time = (E/V)(v/4)
STEP 4: Rate of absorption = (E/V)(v/4)·S·a_avg where a_avg = average absorption coefficient
STEP 5: dE/dt = -(vSa_avg/4V)E
STEP 6: Integrating: E = E₀e^(-t/τ) where τ = 4V/(vSa_avg)
STEP 7: For T, E/E₀ = 10⁻⁶, so e^(-T/τ) = 10⁻⁶
STEP 8: T = τ·ln(10⁶) = (4V/(vSa_avg))·13.8
STEP 9: With v = 344 m/s: T = 0.161V/(Sa_avg)
∴ T = 0.161V/Σ(a_iS_i) ✓ Sabine's formula
Q: A hall has volume 5000 m³ and total absorption 50 m² sabine. Calculate reverberation time.
Summer 2025 3 Marks ⭐ Numerical
💡 Direct application of Sabine's formula.
GIVEN: V = 5000 m³; A = ΣaS = 50 m² sabine
FORMULA: T = 0.161V/A
CALCULATION: T = (0.161 × 5000)/50 = 805/50 = 16.1 s
∴ Reverberation time = 16.1 seconds
Q: Explain the piezoelectric method for production of ultrasonics.
Winter 2024 4 Marks ⭐ Important
💡 Describe the piezoelectric effect and oscillator circuit.
PRINCIPLE: Piezoelectric effect — deformation of certain crystals (quartz, Rochelle salt) when electric field is applied.
CONSTRUCTION: Quartz crystal placed between metal plates connected to LC oscillator.
WORKING: AC voltage causes crystal to expand/contract at oscillator frequency. At resonance, large amplitude ultrasonic waves produced.
RESONANCE CONDITION: f = (1/2t)√(Y/ρ) where t = thickness, Y = Young's modulus, ρ = density
Advantages: High frequency (up to 500 MHz), stable output, efficient
